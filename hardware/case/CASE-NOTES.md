Parametric case v0 for the top-band layout (D1). Generated by
`loudest_case.py` (+ `coupons.py`), all board geometry sourced from
`layout_v2.py`, all mechanical dimensions from
`research/04-mechanical-case-dossier.md` (cited `§…`). Tool: `khana 0.0.2`
(cad-khana), build123d/OCP. **Interference gate: PASS — 0 interferences.**

One CAD, two SKUs (frosted-translucent PETG whole-body glow / black PETG +
diffuser channel) — the split is a **filament swap**, not separate geometry.

**Deck-top-above-lid-bottom stack height = 12.50 mm** (matches the §1A
"case stack math" of 2.4 + 3.5 + 1.6 + 5.0 = 12.5). Add ~3.6 mm bumpon
protrusion → deck sits ~16.1 mm above the desk; ~26 mm to keycap top.

**Under-PCB relief** = 3.5 mm (≥ 2.2 mm bare-air min; in the 3.5–4.0 mm
PORON band, §1A). Socket-to-lid-floor air = **1.65 mm**; the 2 mm PORON is
**die-cut around the sockets** (not modelled as solid).

**Footprint:** outer **89.6 × 109.1 mm**, R8.0; inner cavity 84.8 × 104.3,
R5.3; PCB 84.2 × 103.7, R5.0 with 0.3 mm slip gap per side. (README quotes
~89 × 108.5, which assumes zero PCB gap; the 0.6/0.6 mm difference is the
slip clearance.)

## 3. khana diagnostics summary

`khana check loudest_case.py` → `outputs/mechanism.json`:

- **status: `ok`**, **interferences: 0** (GATE PASS).
- Parts: `top_shell` 27043 mm³, `lid` 17819 mm³, `pcb_board` 13878 mm³,
  `sw_sockets` 2054 mm³ (13 keep-out blocks).
- Assertions (8/8 pass):
  - `no_interference` for all 6 solid pairs (tangent contacts allowed:
    lid bosses↔PCB bottom, sockets↔PCB bottom, lid top↔wall bottom).
  - `clearance top_shell↔pcb_board ≥ 0.25` → 0.30 (PCB slip fit).
  - `clearance sw_sockets↔lid ≥ 1.0` → 1.65 (socket relief).

The PCB proxy is split into `pcb_board` (true 84.2 × 103.7 × 1.6, **R5.0
outline** + 4 corner M3 clearance holes) and `sw_sockets` (13 Kailh keep-out
blocks) so the socket-relief number — which sets the cavity depth — is
asserted directly. Modelling the proxy with sharp corners initially produced
a false 23 mm³ interference at the case centre (four PCB corners poking into
the rounded shell); using the real R5.0 outline resolved it.

`khana check coupons.py` → status `ok`, 0 interferences (5 coupons disjoint).

### Printability (`inspect`, **advisory** — not the gate)
Both printed parts report `ADVISORY`. This is expected and by design:

- **`min_wall` (top_shell 0.09, lid 0.18)** — chamfer-sliver artifacts of the
  0.4 mm EFC chamfers (cad-khana `printability.md`: ray-casting reports false
  sub-0.1 mm walls at chamfered edges). Confirmed: the one coupon without
  chamfers/engraving (`coupon_usb_aperture`) reports an honest 2.0 mm. The
  **governing real thin features are intentional**: 1.2 mm touch window,
  ~1.05 mm clip-shelf ledge, 0.8 mm weight-pocket floor.
- **overhang (top_shell 296 mm², lid 452 mm², max 90°)** — the 45° EFC
  chamfer faces (mission-required, printable at 45°), the 24 mm² USB-cutout
  sill (a <20 mm bridge, fine per §1E), and shallow recess ceilings (bumpon
  recesses, insert bores). All acceptable FDM bridges/45° faces.

The `_advisory_printability` wrapper captures these so they cannot mask the
interference gate; the mechanism `check` remains the sole exit-code gate.

Python sources pass `ruff check` with **zero violations**.

## 4. Print orientation & settings

Material baseline **PETG** (§4: no warp at 90 mm, holds insert preload, PLA
creeps). Frosted-translucent PETG for the glow SKU (same CAD). Slicer
**elephant-foot compensation ~0.15–0.2 mm ON** for every part (in addition to
the CAD 0.4 mm chamfers). 0.4 mm nozzle; +0.1 mm PETG fit modifier already
folded into clearances.

| Part | Orientation | Layer | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **top shell** | **deck FACE-DOWN** (deck at +Z on bed) | 0.12–0.16 mm | clip load in-plane with layers; deck gets bed finish; textured PEI for the molded look (§4). USB sill is a <20 mm bridge — no support. Fuzzy skin on side walls optional. |
| **bottom lid** | **right-side-up** (bottom on bed) | 0.16–0.20 mm | bumpon recesses + insert bores bridge over ≤Ø11.2 — fine. Inserts heat-set from the cavity side. |
| coupon: switch ladder | face-down (like shell) | 0.12–0.16 | 20-min print; pick the cutout that clicks in and holds a firm cap-pull (§3). |
| coupon: insert ladder | right-side-up | 0.16–0.20 | pick the pilot with no burr, insert flush (§4). |
| coupon: USB / enc+JS | face-down | 0.16 | connector/cable + shaft fit. |
| coupon: touch tabs | flat | 0.12 | sense a finger through 1.0/1.2/1.5 mm; pick the window thickness. |

Heat-set inserts: M3 brass (CNC Kitchen L5.7), iron ~245 °C for PETG, melt
90 % then press flat (§4). **Feet sit concentric with the 4 corner bosses**
— the rubber SJ5303 covers the bottom fixing access (standard pattern) and
keeps all feet clear of the SVC service zone.

Corner fixing: **exposed M3 screws at the deck corners** (§6 "tool, not toy"
styling) pass through the deck (Ø3.4), through the PCB corner clearance
holes, into the lid-boss heat-set inserts.

## 6. Open items (resolve before a production freeze)

1. **JS aperture ↔ top-left corner screw overlap (ACTION).** The generous
   Ø16 JS aperture at (13.525, 13.0) overlaps the (7.0, 7.0) corner M3 screw
   clearance hole by **0.84 mm** (centres 8.86 mm apart, JS edge at 8.0 mm).
   The two merge into one opening — the top-left corner fixing is compromised.
   The encoder side (Ø7.5 vs (77.2, 7.0)) is clear by 3.41 mm. **Resolve at
   Phase 1** when the joystick part is chosen: shrink the aperture to the
   real part's travel, or relocate the top-left fixing inboard. The JS
   aperture is deliberately over-sized and provisional until then.
2. **JS aperture / keep-out is Phase-1-provisional** (spec C1: 444 vs 3103 vs
   clone). Aperture, cap pocket (`JS_CAP_POCKET_*`) and travel Ø are
   parameterised generously; freeze against the selected datasheet.
3. **Encoder Ø18 vs Ø20 knob** — band-shift decision at Phase 2 routing
   (notes item 3). Deck opening is only the Ø7.5 shaft pass-through, so the
   knob choice does not change the shell today.
4. **Encoder body height vs deck gap.** EC11E body is 7.5 mm tall (§1C) but
   the deck sits 5.0 mm above the PCB — the body would protrude ~2.5 mm.
   The mission-specified Ø7.5 shaft pass-through is modelled as-is; a
   body-clearance counterbore or a lower-profile mount is needed once the
   encoder part is fixed. (Not in the proxy, so no false interference.)
5. **Steel weight: 3 mm target vs 2.4 mm lid.** Modelled as a 60 × 40 × 1.6
   interior pocket (0.8 mm floor, ~30 g steel). The §4 3 mm bar (~55 g) needs
   local lid thickening in a socket-clear zone, a thinner/wider weight, or
   stacked washers — a one-parameter change (`WEIGHT_POCKET_DEPTH`). The
   60 × 40 footprint overlaps the switch columns, so a solid 3 mm bar cannot
   simply sit in the 3.5 mm cavity (it would foul the sockets).
6. **Battery cavity is a reservation, not a functional pocket.** 50 × 34 is
   marked at 1.5 mm depth; the wireless-fork 6 mm depth requires a deeper
   Pro-SKU lid. `BATTERY_*` parameters carry the footprint forward.
7. **2U stabiliser clearance is approximate** (7.0 × 15.5 relief slots at
   ±11.9). Refine to the exact Cherry plate-mount stab geometry at Phase 2,
   or switch to the 2×1U fallback footprint (notes item 5).
8. **USB-C cutout** uses the §1E flush estimate (10.0 × 4.0). Freeze against
   the EE workstream's chosen receptacle part number.
9. **FR4-plate fallback** (zero-redesign): the PCB outline + origin are
   identical, so an ai03-generated 1.5 mm FR4 plate + sandwich (option #1)
   remains the de-risk path at no cost.
